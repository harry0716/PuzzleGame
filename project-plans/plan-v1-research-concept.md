# Plan V1: Research Concept And Proposal Direction

## Positioning

This project now has enough structure to support early-stage research or project
proposal development, especially in areas related to educational technology,
game-based learning, STEM outreach, technical-vocational exploration, and AI
cross-disciplinary literacy.

At this stage, it is best treated as a research-capable platform with multiple future
proposal directions, rather than forcing one narrow research title too early.

## Why It Has Research Potential

The platform already contains several research-relevant variables:

- multiple scenes across different domains
- module-based routing inside a scene
- difficulty settings
- locked activity control
- presenter-led entry
- scoring-mode variation
- leaderboard and activity segmentation

This means future studies can compare not only participant outcomes, but also how
different activity designs shape those outcomes.

## Possible Research Directions

### 1. Game-Based Learning Effectiveness

Possible question:
How does scene-based interactive learning affect learners' understanding and
engagement in AI and electronics application domains?

### 2. Cross-Disciplinary Technical Literacy

Possible question:
Can a multi-scene platform improve learners' ability to connect AI and electronics
knowledge with real-world sectors such as manufacturing, care, and agriculture?

### 3. Career Exploration And Technical Identity

Possible question:
How do role cards, scenario narratives, and domain-specific scenes affect students'
interest in technical-vocational pathways?

### 4. Difficulty Design And Motivation

Possible question:
How do independent easy / medium / hard scripts influence learner confidence,
challenge perception, and persistence?

### 5. Activity Control And Implementation Efficiency

Possible question:
How do QR diversion, locked entry, module selection, and presenter control influence
event flow, usability, and facilitation quality?

## Potential Data Sources

- scene completion records
- module choice patterns
- difficulty selection patterns
- leaderboard or score distribution by mode
- response behavior by scene or difficulty
- post-activity feedback surveys
- facilitator observation notes
- pre-test / post-test performance if later added

## Potential Proposal Types

- science-popularization grant
- teaching-practice research project
- educational technology action research
- vocational exploration curriculum innovation project
- interdisciplinary STEM outreach proposal
- conference paper or poster based on iterative deployment findings

## Current Advantage

The strongest advantage is that this is not only a concept. It is already a working
platform with iterative design history, multiple implemented scenes, event routing,
and documented development logic. That gives future proposals a stronger practical
foundation than a paper-only idea.

## Near-Term Research Preparation Suggestions

- continue documenting scene and module evolution in `codex.md`
- keep proposal-oriented files versioned in `project-plans/`
- when a stable activity round is run, begin collecting structured reflection data
- identify one or two scenes for early pilot study use
- later add analytics or export-friendly activity summaries if needed
