# Plan V1: AI Cross-Disciplinary Science Popularization

## Positioning

This project can be positioned as an AI cross-disciplinary science-popularization
platform that translates complex technical concepts into interactive, scenario-based
game experiences.

It is no longer just one quiz game. It has evolved into a multi-scene, multi-module,
difficulty-aware event platform that can be used in school outreach, guided visits,
department promotion, public exhibitions, and youth exploration activities.

## Core Value

The project helps learners understand that AI and electronics do not exist only as
abstract theories. They appear in real application domains such as:

- semiconductors and AI chips
- drone control and VR signal transmission
- smart factories and automated inspection
- smart care and long-term care support
- smart agriculture, irrigation, sustainability, and global food-system issues

This cross-domain structure creates a stronger science-popularization effect than a
single-topic activity because participants can see how one technical foundation can
lead into multiple industries and future roles.

## Target Audiences

- junior high school students
- senior high school and vocational high school students
- school visitors and parent groups
- science-popularization activity participants
- youth career-exploration groups

## Why This Project Is Distinctive

- it uses game interaction instead of one-way explanation
- it supports multiple scenes, not one fixed topic
- it supports presenter mode, QR diversion, and locked activity entry
- it supports different difficulty levels for different audiences
- it supports future module-based expansion inside one theme

## Representative Application Scenarios

- department open house
- technical carnival booth activity
- guided campus visit
- subject-exploration workshop
- interdisciplinary science-popularization event
- technology experience station for junior high visitors

## Science-Popularization Narrative

The project can be framed around one central message:

AI and digital systems are not isolated technologies. They connect with industry,
care, agriculture, sustainability, and future career development.

By turning those ideas into playable scenes, the project lowers the entry barrier for
students who may otherwise find electronics, AI, or engineering too abstract.

## Current Evidence Of Maturity

The project already includes:

- multiple complete scenes
- module routing
- difficulty-aware activity flow
- locked event entry
- presenter QR diversion
- leaderboard support
- scene and module expansion capability

This means it already has the foundation of a reusable science-popularization
platform, not just a one-time prototype.

## Future Development Directions

- develop more application-domain scenes
- create independent difficulty scripts for each scene or module
- build outreach packages for different school levels
- produce event manuals and teacher-facing facilitation guides
- build themed editions for exhibitions, summer camps, or guided visits
